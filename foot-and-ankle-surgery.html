Foot & Ankle Surgery

What is Foot & Ankle Surgery?

Foot and Ankle Surgery is often considered the last option after various failed treatments and non-surgical procedures. Foot and Ankle Surgery depends on the state of the condition, the type and severity of the injury, and tendons or ligaments.

When is Foot & Ankle Surgery recommended?

The majority of people who have foot or ankle problems do not require surgery. A number of factors influence the decision to go for an operation, including:

  • How severe your symptoms are
  • Serious ankle pain or foot pain
  • Failed treatments
  • Deformed or broken ankles or feet

Consult regarding the same with your Foot & Ankle Surgery specialists who will be able to tell you whether surgery is a viable option.

How do you prepare for Foot and Ankle Surgery?

Your doctor or nurse will review your general health and provide you with details regarding the preparations for surgery before it takes place. This could include fasting for a few hours prior to your Foot and Ankle Surgery procedures.

You should also talk to your surgeon, anesthetist, or nurse about whether you should stop taking any of your medications or change their dosages before your surgery. If a general anaesthetic is used, a doctor or nurse will examine your general health to ensure that there will be no complications.

You should have a dental check-up as bacteria from dental problems can enter the bloodstream and cause infection.

Advantages of Foot and Ankle Surgery

The following are the major benefits of Foot and Ankle Surgery:

  • Chronic pain relief
  • Improved function and mobility
  • Depending on the procedure, a wider selection of comfortable footwear improves the appearance of your feet.

Foot and Ankle Surgery alternatives

This may well include exercise, footcare, drug treatment, and specially made shoes. Before seeing a surgeon for an operation, most people with foot and ankle problems will receive other treatments from their general physician, rheumatologist, or podiatrist. These could include:

  • Drug treatments, usually painkillers and non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs)
  • Disease-modifying anti-rheumatic drugs (DMARDs) if you have rheumatoid arthritis
  • Steroid injections
  • Suggestions on footwear style
  • Insoles or orthoses as well as specially altered shoes

If these treatments do not relieve your pain, your doctor may then recommend having Foot & Ankle Surgery.

What are the post-surgery complications?

Infections, swelling and stiffness, and wound haemorrhage (bleeding) are all possible complications of Foot and Ankle Surgery. If you maintain good health, the chances of a serious complication from the operation are very low. Complications are avoided at all costs, but they do occur in a few cases. How long does the recovery take?

Foot and Ankle Surgery recovery is entirely dependent on the type of surgery performed. Consult your doctor about the recovery period and any additional measures that may be required.
